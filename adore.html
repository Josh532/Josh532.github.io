Adore

Adore is the fourth studio album by the American rock band The Smashing Pumpkins, released on June 2, 1998. Known for its shift away from the band's earlier, heavier sound, Adore features a more electronic and melancholic atmosphere, incorporating elements of gothic rock, industrial, and ambient music. The album was produced by Billy Corgan and Flood, with the band experimenting with new textures, using drum machines and synthesizers alongside traditional instruments. The album received mixed critical reception initially but has since been recognized for its emotional depth and its exploration of themes like loss, love, and despair. Notable tracks include “Ava Adore,” “Perfect,” and “To Sheila.” Despite not achieving the commercial success of previous releases like Mellon Collie and the Infinite Sadness, Adore has gained a dedicated following over time.
