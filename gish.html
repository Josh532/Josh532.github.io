Gish

Gish, released on May 28, 1991, is the debut studio album by American alternative rock band The Smashing Pumpkins. Produced by Butch Vig, the album blends elements of heavy metal, psychedelia, and shoegaze, marking a key moment in the rise of alternative rock in the early '90s. The album features a mix of intricate guitar work, ethereal vocals, and dense soundscapes. Tracks like “Rhinoceros” and “Siva” introduced the band’s signature sound—layered, dynamic, and often experimental. Gish received critical acclaim for its unique blend of influences and helped establish The Smashing Pumpkins as an innovative force in the alternative music scene. Though it wasn’t an immediate commercial success, the album laid the groundwork for the band's breakthrough with their next release, Siamese Dream (1993).
